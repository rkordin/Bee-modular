The Geometry

01 — Start with a hexagon

Not because it is interesting. Because it is correct.

Six sides. 120-degree corners. Every edge meets its neighbour with zero material wasted. No gaps. No fillers. No tolerance stacked on tolerance hoping things line up.

Hexagons tessellate. They have done this for a very long time.

Why Hexagons

02 — Nature recognised it first

Honeycomb is over a hundred million years old. Bees did not invent it. Physics did. They simply followed the math.

Basalt columns at the Giant's Causeway. Carbon molecules. Turtle shells. The compound eyes of a dragonfly. The same shape appears wherever nature needs to cover area with minimal material and maximal strength.

We did not choose the hexagon. We recognised it.

Load120° angles distribute load across more of the structure
vs. rect90° corners concentrate stress; hexagons spread it
ResultLess material, more strength, better thermal behaviour
ConnectNo adapters, brackets, or compromise between neighbours

The same frame that holds floor-to-ceiling glass also stops a 7.62 mm round.

Living Skin

03 — The skin people live inside

Oak floors, 20 mm thick. Linen on the walls. Brass fittings that age the way brass should — slowly, honestly. Floor-to-ceiling glass where the view earns it. Corten steel outside, rusting on purpose, getting more itself with every season.

Charred timber cladding — Shou Sugi Ban — burned to protect. A 300-year-old Japanese technique applied to a frame built last month.

Nothing here is decorative. The oak is structural dampening. The linen is acoustic. The brass will outlast the owner.

ExteriorTimber cladding, Corten steel, floor-to-ceiling glazing, green roof, solar
Interior20 mm oak flooring, linen acoustic panels, brass hardware, natural stone

Defence Skin

04 — Same frame. Different day.

The outer layer is Armox 500T. Approximately 10.5 mm thick. Hardness around 500 on the Brinell scale. This is the same steel used in armoured personnel carriers. It stops what needs to be stopped.

Behind it, the S355 structural core carries the load. Inside that, a Kevlar anti-spall liner. Three layers. Each one does exactly one job.

Layer 1Armox 500T, ~10.5 mm, ~500 HBW (SSAB, Sweden)
Layer 2S355 structural core, 200 × 200 mm, 12 mm wall
Layer 3Kevlar composite — fragment capture, thermal separation
HVACHEPA H14, overpressure, CBRN options
TargetVPAM PM 9 (ballistic) / STANAG 2280 (blast)
